# Daz Studio Essentials — Curriculum Overview

## Course Philosophy
This is a **focused starter course** that takes a complete beginner from opening Daz Studio for the first time to posing, lighting, and rendering a finished figure — and then carrying that same figure into **Blender** and **Unreal Engine** using the official bridges. The emphasis is the real Daz workflow: assembling scenes from Genesis figures and content, not modeling from scratch. It is designed to pair with the existing Blender and Unreal courses so students can move fluidly across the whole pipeline.

## Who It's For
- Absolute beginners to Daz Studio (no 3D experience required for the core path)
- Blender or Unreal users who want a fast character/figure source for their scenes
- Hobbyists and artists who want photoreal renders and posed characters quickly

## Course Structure
**14 lessons across 5 modules.** Estimated total time: ~12–14 hours. Difficulty ramps from beginner to comfortable-intermediate. Every module ends in a concrete deliverable, and the course closes with a full pipeline capstone.

---

## 📖 MODULE BREAKDOWN

### **Module 1: Getting Started with Daz Studio** (3 lessons)
**Goal:** Install Daz Studio, understand what it is, and get oriented in the interface.

1. **Welcome to Daz Studio** — What Daz is, Genesis figures, what you can make, how it fits alongside Blender & Unreal, licensing/cost, system requirements
2. **Installing Daz & the Content Library** — Daz Install Manager (DIM), Daz Central, Daz Connect, Smart Content vs Content Library, free starter content, where files live
3. **The Daz Studio Interface** — Viewport navigation, the Scene pane, Parameters pane, Content Library/Smart Content, tabs, layouts, and saving your workspace

**Module Deliverable:** A working Daz install with starter content loaded and a customized, comfortable interface layout.

---

### **Module 2: Figures — Posing, Shaping & Surfaces** (3 lessons)
**Goal:** Load a Genesis figure and make it *yours* — posed, shaped, and surfaced.

4. **Loading & Posing Genesis Figures** — Loading Genesis 8/9, pose presets, ActivePose & PowerPose, manual bone rotation, pinning, saving poses
5. **Shaping Characters with Morphs** — The Shaping pane, character/head/body morphs, dialing custom faces and bodies, expressions, mixing morphs responsibly
6. **Materials & Iray Surfaces** — The Surfaces pane, the Iray Uber shader, applying material presets, skin/eyes/hair surfaces, quick tweaks

**Module Deliverable:** A custom-shaped, posed character with polished surfaces, saved as a scene subset.

---

### **Module 3: Scenes, Lighting & Rendering** (3 lessons)
**Goal:** Turn a figure into a finished image.

7. **Building a Scene** — Environments and props, hair & clothing, cameras, framing and composition, headlamp vs scene lights
8. **Lighting for Iray** — HDRI dome lighting, emissive surfaces, photometric (point/spot) lights, three-point setups in Daz, tone mapping basics
9. **Rendering with Iray** — Render Settings, samples & convergence, the denoiser, spot-render, canvases, saving and light post-processing

**Module Deliverable:** A fully lit, Iray-rendered portrait or scene exported as a finished image.

---

### **Module 4: Simulation & Animation** (2 lessons)
**Goal:** Add motion and realistic cloth to your work.

10. **dForce Cloth & Hair Simulation** — Dynamic vs conforming clothing, running a dForce simulation, simulation settings, dForce hair, fixing explosions & pokethrough
11. **Animation Basics with the Timeline** — Keyframes, the Timeline vs aniMate2/aniBlocks, a simple turntable, rendering an image sequence to video

**Module Deliverable:** A short animated turntable of your character with simulated clothing.

---

### **Module 5: The Bridge Workflow — Daz to Blender & Unreal** (3 lessons)
**Goal:** Take your Daz figure into the tools from the Blender and Unreal courses.

12. **The Daz to Blender Bridge** — Installing the bridge, exporting a Genesis figure, what transfers (mesh, rig, materials), fixing up materials in Blender, when to use Diffeomorphic instead
13. **The Daz to Unreal Bridge** — Installing the UE plugin + bridge, exporting a figure, skeleton & retargeting, materials in UE, animation transfer, how this relates to MetaHuman
14. **Capstone: A Full Pipeline Portfolio Piece** — Pose, shape, surface, and render a character in Daz, then bridge the *same* figure into Blender and Unreal; wrap-up, portfolio tips, and where to go next

**Course Deliverable:** One character rendered in Daz Studio **and** placed in a Blender scene **and** dropped into an Unreal level — a single asset proving you can move across the whole pipeline.

## 🔗 Cross-Course Integration
- **Blender course** — Lessons on materials, lighting, and rendering transfer directly; the Blender Bridge (Lesson 12) lands figures into the Blender skills students already have.
- **Unreal course** — The Unreal Bridge (Lesson 13) feeds characters into levels; ties into the Unreal course's actors, materials, and animation lessons.

## 🎯 Prerequisites
- A computer that meets Daz Studio's requirements (dedicated GPU strongly recommended for Iray)
- A free Daz 3D account
- No prior 3D experience required; Blender/Unreal familiarity helps for Module 5 but isn't required
